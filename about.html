About

Why this work — and why me.

Complex policy issues should not belong only to experts. Democracy is only as strong as the people who understand and participate in it. That conviction is the through-line behind everything on this site.

"My work focuses on sense-making around European politics and economy, improving democracy, and promoting human and social rights. Complex policy issues should be accessible to everyone."
🧭

Sense-making

Breaking down EU institutions, political economy, and international dynamics into language anyone can engage with — from newsletters to short-form video.

🗳️

Improving democracy

Closing the gap between citizens and the institutions that govern them — through advocacy, coalition building, and civic education.

⚖️

Human & social rights

From Sudan to civic space in Europe — making sure rights stay at the centre of policy decisions, not in the footnotes.

The story

Campaign builder.
Sense-maker. Reluctant expert.

I'm a policy and advocacy professional with 10+ years of experience across civil society, EU-level advocacy, and political movements. Right now I'm a policy officer at The Good Lobby Italia, where I focus on democratic engagement, transparency, and direct lobbying — while also training others to do the same.

My previous work had me pushing EU institutions to act on gender-based violence in Sudan, human trafficking in Tunisia and Libya, and more. Before that: more campaigns, more coalition work, more trying to make institutions actually listen.

My core interests have always been science (especially evolution), international relations, history and anthropology. I started as a chemist and economic historian — and those backgrounds still shape how I read the world.

I founded The Continental Brief — a newsletter on EU politics and political economy — because complex issues should belong to everyone, not just insiders.
