About me

Campaign builder.
Sense-maker. Reluctant expert.

I'm a policy and advocacy professional with 10+ years of experience across civil society, EU-level advocacy, and political movements. Right now I'm a policy officer at The Good Lobby Italia, where I focus on democratic engagement, transparency, and direct lobbying — while also training others to do the same.

My previous work had me pushing EU institutions to act on gender-based violence in Sudan, human trafficking in Tunisia and Libya, and more. Before that: more campaigns, more coalition work, more trying to make institutions actually listen.

My core interests have always been science (especially evolution), international relations, history and anthropology. I started as a chemist and economic historian — and those backgrounds still shape how I read the world. I'm also an avid reader, sometimes a gamer, and I love my comics.

I founded The Continental Brief — a newsletter on EU politics and political economy — because complex issues should belong to everyone, not just insiders.

Mission & vision

Why this work matters.

Complex policy issues should not belong only to experts. Democracy is only as strong as the people who understand and participate in it.

"My work focuses on sense-making around European politics and economy, improving democracy, and promoting human and social rights. I believe complex policy issues should be accessible to everyone."
🧭

Sense-making

Breaking down the complexity of EU institutions, political economy, and international dynamics into language anyone can engage with. From newsletters to short-form video, I translate the machinery of power into accessible insight.

🗳️

Improving democracy

Working to close the gap between citizens and the institutions that govern them. Through advocacy, coalition building, and civic education, I push for accountable, transparent, and participatory democratic systems.

⚖️

Human & social rights

From Sudan to civic space in Europe, I work to ensure human rights remain at the centre of policy decisions. Amplifying voices, building coalitions, and holding institutions to account.
